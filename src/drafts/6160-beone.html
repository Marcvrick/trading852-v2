In November 2023, a clinical trial called ALPINE produced results that landed with unusual force. The trial tested whether BRUKINSA, made by a Chinese biotechnology company called BeOne Medicines, could outperform Imbruvica, a blood cancer drug from AbbVie that had generated more than US$5 billion in annual revenue at its peak. BRUKINSA won on both measures that mattered: better progression-free survival and a cleaner safety profile. A company with no commercial drug a decade earlier had beaten one of the dominant franchises in blood cancer treatment using superior clinical data.

BeOne Medicines (6160.HK) is that company. Its net margin on US$5.3 billion in FY2025 revenue was 5.4%.

That gap is the thesis.

What BeOne Does

BeOne Medicines develops and commercialises cancer treatments. It is not a laboratory trying to reach commercialisation: it operates in over 75 countries and maintains a direct sales force in the United States and Europe.

Its main product is BRUKINSA (zanubrutinib), a BTK inhibitor. A BTK inhibitor works by blocking a signalling protein that blood cancer cells rely on to survive. In FY2025, BRUKINSA generated US$3.9B in sales, up 48%. It is the fastest-growing drug of its type in the world.

The second product is TEVIMBRA (tislelizumab), a checkpoint inhibitor approved in 51 countries for multiple cancer types. It generated US$737M in FY2025, up 18%. Its most important US indications are still moving through the approval pipeline.

In China, Amgen chose BeOne as its commercial distribution partner. Amgen operates across more than 100 countries. That choice is a form of validation that does not appear on a balance sheet.

In FY2025, the company recorded its first-ever annual net profit: US$287M on US$5.3B in total revenue.

A Price Built for Continuity

At HKD 187.90, the stock trades at approximately 30 times its projected FY2026 earnings. That is where growth-stage oncology companies trade. AstraZeneca, one of the largest pharmaceutical companies in the world, trades at roughly 16 times: its major drugs are mature and its growth is measured. Blueprint Medicines, a US oncology company at a comparable development stage, trades at approximately 30 times.

BeOne is priced at the median of growth oncology. There is no discount. The question is whether the earnings beneath that multiple compound faster than the market expects.

In the first year of profitability, the net margin is 5.4%. Mature pharmaceutical companies run at 25 to 30 percent net margin. The franchise is priced in. The margin it will produce has not been.

Three Reasons the Margin Is Moving

The operating leverage is mechanical. Three forces are driving it simultaneously.

  1. 01 R&D intensity is declining. Building BRUKINSA cost more than US$4 billion in cumulative losses. That investment produced the clinical data, the manufacturing processes, and the regulatory approvals for 75 markets. The core infrastructure is built. New R&D now targets additional indications and next-generation combinations, not the foundational platform. Revenue is compounding at 40%+. R&D spending is not growing at the same rate. The ratio moves.
  2. 02 The commercial infrastructure does not scale with revenue. BeOne spent years building a US oncology sales force, obtaining European regulatory approvals, and developing the hospital and prescriber relationships that convert approvals into prescriptions. That fixed cost is in place. The next billion dollars of BRUKINSA revenue costs materially less to generate than the first billion did. That is the nature of a mature distribution network deployed against a still-growing product.
  3. 03 The timeline is public. Q1 2026 revenue is reported in May. FY2026 annual results arrive in March 2027. If BRUKINSA quarterly revenue reaches US$1.1B in Q1, it is on trajectory for US$4.5B+ annualised. These are not remote projections. They are quarterly checkpoints against a model that has been running ahead of analyst consensus.

BeOne's first annual profit arrived in FY2025. Analyst consensus had placed profitability in FY2026 or FY2027. It arrived twelve months early. The cost structure peaked before the models expected it to.

What the Business Is Worth

At HKD 187.90, EV/FCF stands at approximately 34.7 times. Growth oncology peers trade between 30 and 35 times free cash flow. BeOne sits at the upper end of that range.

The floor has a number: US$3.5B in net cash. The business generated US$942M in free cash flow in FY2025. It is entirely self-funding.

Risks We Are Not Downplaying

Two risks are real and documented.

Risk 1: Pirtobrutinib (Eli Lilly's Jaypirca)

Eli Lilly's pirtobrutinib is a next-generation BTK inhibitor approved for patients who have already failed a first-line BTK therapy. As of FY2025, pirtobrutinib and BRUKINSA serve different patient populations. Pirtobrutinib is indicated for treatment-resistant cases. BRUKINSA is the preferred first-line option. The risk is forward-looking: if Eli Lilly generates head-to-head clinical data showing pirtobrutinib superiority in treatment-naive patients, it would be a direct challenge to BRUKINSA's core market. No such trial is currently underway. Eli Lilly has the capital and the motivation to design one.

Risk 2: Valuation priced for execution

At 30 times forward earnings, the current price requires BRUKINSA to continue growing above 40% and operating leverage to deliver on schedule. Both must be true simultaneously. A single quarter of BRUKINSA growth decelerating below 30% would likely produce a significant re-rating, even if the drug remained clinically superior. The history of growth pharma contains multiple drugs that dethroned incumbents and then de-rated when their growth slowed from exceptional to merely strong. That risk is structural in any quality growth situation priced at a premium multiple.

A third factor operates in the background: BRUKINSA's revenue is generated globally, but BeOne's cost structure and reporting currency are US dollar-denominated. Chinese mainland competitors are investing heavily in me-too BTK inhibitors. None have demonstrated clinical superiority comparable to BRUKINSA, but price competition in China's domestic oncology market is intensifying. BeOne's positioning as a clinically differentiated premium product is its defence. The ALPINE data is the moat.

The Decision

Written at HKD 187.90 on April 26, 2026. Verdict: Monitor.

The thesis is intact. The business is genuine. The current price is not compelling enough.

At HKD 187.90, EV/FCF of ~34.7× sits at the ceiling of the growth oncology peer range. Forward P/E of ~30.4× prices in full FY2026 delivery of approximately US$1.2B in net income: a jump from US$287M in a single year. One year into profitability, with the operating leverage still to be demonstrated in quarterly filings, that leaves no room for delay and no buffer against competitive surprise.

At HKD ~132, the picture is different. EV/FCF falls to approximately 24×, below the 30–35× growth oncology peer floor. Forward P/E falls to approximately 21×, below Blueprint Medicines and most comparable growth-stage peers. The same franchise, the same operating leverage thesis, but at a 30% discount to today's price and a 25–30% discount to where peers trade.
