HKEX filings. Mispriced companies. Written up when the math holds.

Who I am

Reading annual reports for years. At some point started writing things down, then sharing them. Trading852 is that.

Why Hong Kong

Because it's one of the few markets where doing the work still gives you an edge.

The institutional coverage on mid-cap HK conglomerates is thin. The structures are complex enough that most people don't bother. And because the market has been in the news for the wrong reasons since 2021, a lot of quality businesses are priced as if the discount is permanent.

I don't think it always is. I try to find the ones where it isn't.

What I actually do

I read filings. I build sum-of-parts valuations. I look for a gap between what a company owns and what the market is paying for it — and then I look for a reason that gap might close within a defined window. A buyback, a disposal, a privatisation offer. Something that forces the price to move.

When I find something that holds up under scrutiny, I write about it here.

What this is not

This is not investment advice. I'm not telling you what to buy. I'm sharing my research because writing forces me to think clearly — and because I'd rather read research that's honest about its assumptions than one that pretends the risks don't exist.

Make your own decisions. Read the disclaimer before you act on anything here.
