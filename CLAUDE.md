# CLAUDE.md – Trading852-v2

## Publishing Rules

### ⚠️ MANDATORY: Internal Linking on Every Article

**Rule:** Every article published to `publish/analyses/` MUST include at least one internal link to another Trading852 article before push.

**Why:** Internal links improve SEO (crawlability, authority flow), reduce bounce rate, and guide readers through related content. A single-article publish with no cross-reference is incomplete.

**How to apply:**
1. When writing an article, identify 1-2 related pieces (same category, adjacent market thesis, same ticker, complementary analysis).
2. Add a link near the end of the article body:
   - Use `<a href="/analyses/slug">Article Title &rarr;</a>` (forward link)
   - Or add a reciprocal link in the related article pointing back (bidirectional)
3. Label clearly: "Continue reading: [Title]" or "Related: [Title]" or "Previous: [Title]"
4. Before pushing, verify the linked article exists and its URL is correct.

**Build validation:** The build.js will warn if an article has zero internal links to other published articles (check the console output).

**Edge cases:**
- Market-thesis hubs (like market-thesis.html) are exempt—they aggregate cards, not articles.
- Articles can link to sector hubs or static pages, but should prefer linking to sibling analyses.

### Article Structure

Every article must have:
- CONFIG with `pubDate`, `ogTitle`, `articleSection`, `description`
- JSONLD schema block (Article type)
- Article hero (breadcrumb, metadata, title)
- Article body with at least one internal cross-reference
- No orphaned articles: if it stands alone with zero inbound/outbound links, it fails the review

### SEO & Linking Best Practices

- Link text should be descriptive, not generic ("Read the analysis" ✓, "click here" ✗)
- Prefer linking to articles in the same `articleSection` or adjacent sections
- Avoid mutual loops: A → B → A is acceptable, but A → B → A → B is redundant
- Links should appear late in the article (end of body) to not distract from the core thesis
