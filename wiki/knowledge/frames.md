# Analytical Frames

Part of the [Knowledge Layer](index.md). The reusable reasoning patterns, extracted from the eighteen published articles. All DURABLE: a frame does not expire when its numbers do.

[hong-kong-discount-cheap-two-ways.html](../../publish/analyses/hong-kong-discount-cheap-two-ways.html) is the site's explicit methodological piece. The master frame is F1 and most stock articles are instances of it.

## The master frame

### F1. Cheap is a question, not an answer

A discount is information only once paired with two answers: does the business earn a real return on the money it uses, and do the owners actually return cash. The bargain and the value trap are identical on a screen. Every single-name valuation piece descends from this.

Source: [Cheap Is a Question, Not an Answer](../../publish/analyses/hong-kong-discount-cheap-two-ways.html), 2026-06-21.

### F5. The discount is earned

The counterweight F1 requires. Three structural reasons a low price is the correct price: cash trapped behind capital controls is worth less than cash that can leave, policy can rewrite a company's economics overnight, and the local habit of returning cash has historically been weak. Required in any bullish HK valuation piece.

Worked cases: CK Asset (about 2% on reinvested capital, below cost of capital, profit down more than a third over three years, no buyback), Yuexiu Property (attributable profit down about 95%, dividend more than ten times earnings, "not a yield, a countdown").

### F6. Cheap, real, and locked

The third verdict, and the site's own admission that the two-column table cannot hold every case. The business clears the return test, the cash clears the screen, and a controlling holder decides whether a minority shareholder ever sees it. Correct verdict is MONITOR until a catalyst appears: a buyback actually executed, a clean offer at or above asset value, or a public commitment to return the cash.

Worked cases: Dickson Concepts (Poon family 60.5%), Emperor Watch and Jewellery (family near 60%, buyback mandate never used).

## Hidden assets and residual value

### F2. Net cash as free option

Cash and securities at or above market cap, so the operating business is priced at zero or less, with the business separately shown to be profitable. The site's signature construction.

Strongest version: Dickson Concepts, net cash HKD 2,729M against a HKD 2,354M market cap, enterprise value negative HKD 375M. "The market is paying you HKD 375 million to buy this company."

**Why it persists (the float mechanic):** the controller holds 60.5%, leaving a float too thin for the institutions with the analytical capacity to find the situation to enter and exit at scale. A market that overlooks a negative enterprise value is not pricing risk, it is pricing indifference. This is the standing explanation for why any HK deep-value screen result stays cheap.

### F2b. The controller's offer is a ceiling, not a floor

**Read this before citing any privatization offer as evidence of value.** An offer at HKD 7.20 against net cash per share of HKD 7.08 is an offer at roughly cash value: the family takes the whole profitable operating business for close to nothing rather than closing the gap for every holder. The offer reads as a ceiling on generosity.

This frame reverses the April 2026 reading in the same article, which argued the offer was an empirically demonstrated floor. Both readings remain live in the published file. See [open-questions.md](open-questions.md) item 17 before writing anything that touches it.

Source: [Dickson Concepts](../../publish/analyses/0113-dickson-concepts.html) update, 2026-06-22.

### F2c. Sum-of-parts: net cash plus listed stakes

Strip net cash and marked-to-market listed stakes out of equity value, then price what remains. Tencent: strip RMB 147B net cash and RMB 708B of listed stakes out of HK$4.1 trillion and the operating business trades at about 11x EV/EBIT.

Three clauses travel with it, and the third is not optional:

- **Why the market discounts a stake portfolio:** minority stakes generate no cash the parent can spend. Dividends are small, gains are on paper, the holder is under no obligation to sell, and selling RMB 708B of minority positions into the open market would take years and move prices against the seller.
- **Marked to market caps the downside argument:** a decline in the underlying already sits inside the carrying value the same day, in public, with no write-down to argue over.
- **A marked portfolio is a bonus, not a floor.** It is a live mark that moves with the holdings, held in mind as a bonus on the operating business, not as value the price is quietly owed. Never write a hidden-asset piece that treats the asset as a floor.

**The counter-example that must travel with it:** Prosus owns about 23% of Tencent, a stake that dwarfs the rest of its assets, and has traded at a wide discount to that single holding for years. The upside does not depend on the market changing its mind, and Prosus shows the discount can sit unrecognized for years.

### F2d. Third-party transaction as the price benchmark

An arm's-length strategic payment prices the residual. AstraZeneca paid US$100M upfront cash for one Phase I molecule while the entire company traded at about US$730M; net of HK$1.73B cash, the market valued an approved cancer drug, a Phase III trial, and up to US$1.9B of AZ milestones at US$507M.

**Deal structure is the signal, not the headline number.** Most pharma licensing is small upfront plus large milestones. AZ paid US$100M cash before any pivotal data existed, and the US$1.9B of downstream milestones is the larger signal, because AZ only pays those if it actively chooses to keep running trials.

### F2e. The in-kind distribution, and its three conditions

The cleanest way to surface locked stake value when: the stake has matured, the parent no longer needs the capital, and the market gives the position no credit.

Precedent: December 2021, Tencent distributed its entire JD.com stake, roughly 457 million shares worth about HK$127 billion, directly to shareholders in specie. No sale into the market, no block discount, no corporate tax drag. Its JD stake fell from about 17% to roughly 2%. A second precedent exists (UMG to TME shareholders, early 2025) and the Tencent article does not cite it. See [open-questions.md](open-questions.md) item 20.

## Reading the numbers

### F3. Peer-multiple gap

Same industry, comparable quality, half the multiple, source and date attached. The published sets live in [peer-multiples.md](peer-multiples.md) and should not be re-derived.

**The implied-price ladder:** convert each peer multiple into an implied price for the subject, and include the half-the-median rung, which separates a structural re-rating from a simple discount normalisation. Tencent Music: Spotify about 40x implies about HKD 200, peer median about 25x implies about HKD 125, half the peer median about 12.5x implies about HKD 62, against HKD 35. "Reaching half the peer median requires only that the stock leave the deepest part of the China tech discount."

### F3b. The discount is on one multiple, not every multiple

The honesty clause. Galaxy trades at about 8.6x EV/EBITDA, a sector low, but on EV/EBIT, which counts the heavy depreciation a resort operator carries, it sits near 11x, in line with the peer average rather than below it. The discount is on the EBITDA line and in the balance sheet, not on every multiple.

Never let one flattering multiple stand alone. Check whether the discount survives the basis change.

### F3c. A forward-guess multiple is not a multiple

Alibaba's "about 11 times operating profit" was a forward guess made before profit collapsed. On the actual full-year numbers it was closer to 31 times. Before citing any forward EV/EBIT, state whose forecast the denominator is. The site has been burned by exactly this.

### F8. Two numbers, one is a distraction

The headline metric collapses while the driver metric is intact, and the article separates them. Prada (EPS down 74%, revenue up 9%), Alibaba (EBITA down 57%, cloud up 36%), Tencent Music (profit up 66%, stock down 66%).

Three mechanics do the separating:

- **Strip the one-off gain.** Alibaba's reported RMB 102.1B profit contained RMB 87.5B of one-off investment gains; the repeatable profit fell 62%.
- **Normalise before multiplying.** Galaxy's reported FY2025 EBITDA of HK$14.5B included about HK$1.5B of favourable VIP hold variance; normalised is HK$13.0B, which moves the multiple from 8.6x to 9.6x and changes neither the cash position nor the dividend. Generalises to: identify the non-repeatable component before applying any multiple.
- **An accounting charge is not an earnings miss.** Prada's EPS fell 74% on Versace goodwill, restructuring and advisory fees in the year the deal closed; underlying earnings fell 5%.

### F4. The market prices it as an X company

Name the identity the price implies, then show the asset that contradicts it. Midea (priced as a washing-machine company, owns one of the four big robot makers), Yadea (the market sees a bicycle company), Alibaba (this is what Amazon looked like in 2014).

**The mechanism:** a non-core asset folded into one segment line is invisible to the specialist investors who would value it and discounted by the generalists who own the stock.

### F4b. The ROIC distortion

Headline return on capital reads badly because idle cash sits in the capital base. Midea reports about 8%; excluding idle cash and measuring only capital tied up in factories, brands and inventory, the business earns more than 20%. The reusable explanation for any net-cash-heavy name that screens badly.

## Capital returns and management signals

### F7. Return of capital as the catalyst

The move begins with the names handing cash back. Paired with flow evidence (foreign long-only net buying, Southbound records).

### F7b. An authorisation is not an execution

Never count an authorised buyback as capital returned. Jacobio's board authorised up to HK$100M and deployed HK$3.2M, 3.2%. The catalyst that counts is a buyback actually executed.

### F7c. Repurchase average as a price reference

A company's own executed repurchase average is a transacted benchmark, unlike an authorisation. Tencent spent HK$80 billion on buybacks last year at an average of HK$521.55; the stock last traded at HK$456.20 on July 14, more than 12% below that average.

### F7d. Buyback at peak capex is a management statement

US$12 billion of buyback authorization active at the same time capital spending is at a multi-year peak. A company that thought its stock was overpriced would not do both. Weigh against F7b.

### F7e. Insider buying that stops

Read the absence of follow-on buying. A CEO who spent HK$96M of his own money at an average HK$8.56 and then made no further purchases in six months while sitting on a 16% loss is a cooling signal: a CEO with conviction and capital at risk typically buys more at a discount.

### F7f. Disclosure decisions move multiples before operations change

Cuts both ways. Beginning to report a high-margin layer separately (as AWS did with Bedrock from 2024) lets the market model it in isolation, and that single decision can drive multiple expansion before any operational change. Withdrawing a metric is the mirror image and is an adverse minority act: TME moved paying subscribers and ARPPU to annual-only from Q1 2026, so the company stopped disclosing the very numbers that would prove or disprove the bear case in real time.

## Structure and risk

### F9. Scenario triptych plus invalidation

Every article closes on three rows maximum (Scenario, Observable signal, Implication), then two or three concrete exit triggers, then the lapidary sentence.

**The sensitivity grid variant:** per-share, never per-multiple. Add net cash into the enterprise-value bridge and divide by shares in issue. Read down a column and the growth adds up; read across a row and the re-rating does.

**Probability weights for a pre-profit name:** value each component separately (cash needs no assumption and is verifiable in audited accounts; the commercial drug at a P/S multiple on peak revenue; the rest probability-weighted). If that feels too optimistic, the cash component alone tells you the floor.

### F9b. Name the filing and the date that resolves it

Structural habit across every article: the central uncertainty gets a dated, filing-based resolution point, not a vague "we will see".

### F9c. Design invalidation on the variable the thesis rides

The site's documented miss. Alibaba's original sell-signals watched the balance sheet, and the balance sheet held. They missed free cash flow turning negative, which was the variable that mattered. The replacement test names it: MONITOR until free cash flow turns positive and holds for two quarters running.

### F9d. Dual-catalyst dependency, and correlated risks

When both conditions must be true simultaneously, the thesis is harder than a single-catalyst bet. And risks are rarely independent: if Phase 4 slips and GGR growth decelerates together, the base case converges toward the bear case. State the correlation rather than listing risks as independent.

### F9e. Net cash is a weapon, not a cushion, and it costs beta

The honest two-sided version. In a downturn the levered operators conserve cash and stop spending while the net-cash operator keeps building. The reverse holds in a strong recovery, when levered names carry more upside and re-rate faster. Net cash is the defensive way to own a cycle, not the highest-beta one. Cite the caveat, not just the weapon half.

### F9f. Opacity is priced as impairment

The market is penalising the opacity, not the performance. An undisclosed segment or an unquantified exposure gets priced as a confirmed impairment. Distinguish an unquantified risk from a confirmed one.

## Reading the tape and the narrative

### F10. Structural-level frame

Every claim about past indicator behaviour anchored to date, observable price, and measured outcome. Method and conventions in [levels.md](levels.md).

### F10b. Buy the reclaim, not the fall

In a post-correction high-volatility regime, a stop calibrated to calm markets gets swept on noise when the asset can move several percent in a day. Consistent across the gold article and the SPY failed-retest work.

### F10c. The de-rating with no event

A slow de-rating is the harder kind to read, because no single event carries it. Galaxy topped at HK$43.13 on Feb 24 and fell in stages to HK$28.68 by June 26 with no crash, no profit warning, no revenue miss, and had already shed about 18% before the April 2 tariff round. The multiple did the falling, not the business. Test the tape's causal story against the dates before accepting a narrative.

### F10d. Test the transmission channel before accepting a macro narrative

Macau casinos make no goods and appear in no tariff schedule. The transmission from a US trade measure runs through mainland household income over quarters, not through a customs border on the day of an announcement. That indirect risk is real and is the central one, but it is not a mechanical link that explains a multiple falling by a third. The required move before attributing any HK selloff to tariffs.

Contrast with the case where the link is mechanical: Temu ran on the US de minimis exemption that Washington scrapped in 2025.

### F10e. What consensus feared versus what the data showed

The two-column table. Each named fear set against the reported data. Then the hedge that keeps it honest: the bear case is not in the right-hand column, it is a forecast that sits twelve months out.

### F10f. Explain a one-off with a named, dated cause

June Macau GGR fell 12.1% to MOP 18.5B, attributed by operators and Citi to the FIFA World Cup: one month down, on a known one-off, against a half still growing at +6.8%. The template for isolating a calendar one-off from a trend break, and for naming the forward test that resolves it.

### F11. Macro-frame articles

A single mechanism, tracked live. No ticker, so excluded from the scorecard by construction. The peg to HK prices, rates to financials, real rates and the dollar to gold, GDP to shareholder returns. Detail in [macro-hsi.md](macro-hsi.md), [financials-rates.md](financials-rates.md), [gold.md](gold.md).

## Competitive analysis

### F12. The competitor is also a paying customer

ByteDance is simultaneously a subscription competitor (Soda Music) and a licensing customer (Douyin), and the licensing line grew 39.2% in 2025, faster than any plausible rate at which Soda Music could shrink the paying base. The competitor licenses the music; the market prices the licensee like the casualty.

### F12b. Separate engagement from monetisation before pricing a threat

Soda Music's MAU scale is real and disclosed, but ByteDance has never disclosed paying subscribers for it. The market priced the threat as if conversion were already a given, and the data is still missing.

### F12c. Test the moat typology before accepting commoditisation

AI-disruption risk sits on roughly a tenth of Tencent's portfolio, the ad-driven discovery platforms, not the whole of it. A marketplace's moat is its merchants and delivery fleet, a game's is its players and catalogue, a video platform's is the creators already posting there. None of that is a few thousand lines of code.

## Editorial

### F13. The published call that did not work

Three are documented as such: Alibaba (CONVICTION to MONITOR, the HK$165 to 200 target missed), Dickson (CONVICTION to MONITOR on a reversed reading of the same evidence), Tencent Music (both bull triggers missed, base case held). The willingness to publish these is itself a durable editorial pattern, and F9c and F3c are the lessons extracted from them.

### F14. The SEO-correction section

Capture search traffic on a false premise while correcting it. Jacobio: a widely-searched Novartis USP1 deal that does not exist, stated plainly as a negative, with the likely confusion explained and the real deal supplied.
