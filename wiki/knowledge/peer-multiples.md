# Published Peer Multiples

Part of the [Knowledge Layer](index.md). Every peer set the site has published, with its date, basis, and source.

**All PERISHABLE. Re-verify every figure live before it enters an article** ([index.md](index.md), the hard rule). These sets exist so a future article does not re-derive a comparison the site already owns, and so it does not silently publish a different number for the same company.

**Basis discipline.** Each table states its basis. EV/EBIT is not P/E is not EV/EBITDA. Three of the collisions in [open-questions.md](open-questions.md) are basis errors rather than data errors.

## Luxury, EV/EBIT

| Company | Multiple |
|---|---|
| Hermes | about 30x |
| Richemont | about 18x |
| LVMH | about 15x |
| Kering | about 11x |
| Prada (1913.HK) | 9.2x (-39%) |

as_of 2026-04-12. Source: [Prada](../../publish/analyses/1913-prada.html). Data: "Luxury peer EV/EBIT multiples: Bloomberg consensus, April 2026".

**Two live problems in this set.** The same article says "Hermes trades at 40x" in its body, ten turns above its own table, with no basis stated. And it defines the luxury sector floor as 15x while listing Kering, a large-cap luxury operator, at about 11x. See [open-questions.md](open-questions.md) items 9 and 10.

**HK luxury peers** trade at 8x to 15x EV/EBIT (as_of 2026-04-11, [Dickson](../../publish/analyses/0113-dickson-concepts.html)). Unsourced. Verify before leaning on it.

**Published drawdown precedents (DURABLE):** Kering traded 10x to 11x EV/EBIT at peak pessimism in 2024 before recovering to 14x to 18x. LVMH fell 40% in 2022 to 2023 on China fears before recovering. The "luxury recoveries start here" evidence base.

## US and China tech, EV/EBIT

| Company | Multiple |
|---|---|
| Amazon | 35x |
| Microsoft | about 25x |
| Google | 19x |
| Tencent | 15x |
| Alibaba | 10.8x (-28%) |

as_of April 2026. Source: [Alibaba](../../publish/analyses/9988-alibaba.html). Data: Bloomberg consensus, April 2026.

**Three problems.** The table is titled EV/EBIT but the body calls Amazon's 35x "forward earnings", which is P/E ([open-questions.md](open-questions.md) item 13). The Tencent article restates Tencent at 13.5x and Alibaba at 10.1x a month later, neither cross-referencing the other (items 11 and 12). And Alibaba's 10.8x was a forward guess whose denominator missed by roughly 3x ([frames.md](frames.md) F3c).

**China internet peers** (as_of May 2026, [Tencent](../../publish/analyses/0700-tencent.html)): Tencent headline 13.5x, 11.0x ex-portfolio; Alibaba 10.1x; NetEase 12.4x; sector average 15.6x. Data: HKEX filings FY2025 and Q1 2026, peer market data May 2026.

Note the sector average here (15.6x) sits almost exactly where the Alibaba article put Tencent itself (15x).

**Macau multiples are published as a band, not per name**, and the article says so explicitly: the sector runs 8x to 10x EV/EBITDA, with Galaxy at 8.6x on the 2026-07-10 close.
