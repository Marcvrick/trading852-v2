## The peg (DURABLE)

The canonical statement. These are fixed policy numbers, not drifting quotes.

- Since October 1983 the Hong Kong dollar has been pinned to the US dollar at close to 7.78 under the Linked Exchange Rate System, a currency board in which every HK dollar in circulation is fully backed by USD reserves held by the HKMA. Data: HKMA, "How the Linked Exchange Rate System works", accessed July 2026.
- The convertibility band runs 7.75 to 7.85. At 7.75 the HKMA sells HKD and loosens the banking system; at 7.85 it buys HKD back and tightens it. Data: HKMA LERS page.
- The currency board means Hong Kong has no independent interest rate policy. It cannot cut to support a slowing local economy while the Fed hikes, the way the ECB or BoJ can. This is the one-sentence frame for why a US macro print moves HK equities with no local news.

**The tightening chain**, the reusable causal spine. Cite the chain, re-verify only the levels:

Fed hawkish, US bills out-yield HK deposits with no FX risk, traders sell HKD, the rate drifts to 7.85, the HKMA buys HKD, the Aggregate Balance drains, HIBOR climbs, HKD-funded companies pay more.

The carry trade driving it is arithmetic rather than speculation: borrow HKD, convert to USD, hold short-term US paper for the spread, with the peg removing currency risk. Once the gap exists, capital moves automatically. This is the mechanical alternative to any account that attributes HK outflows to sentiment.

**The second channel runs direct to valuations.** Rising US Treasury yields raise the discount rate for every stock on earth, and long-duration growth names lose most because more of their expected cash sits years out. Hang Seng Tech is close to 40% of the index and leans heavily toward long-dated earnings, which is why the HSI is more rate-sensitive than its old-economy reputation implies.

Do not conflate the two 40% claims about the same index: Hang Seng Tech close to 40% (rate duration) and financials roughly 40% ([financials-rates.md](financials-rates.md)). Different objects, both published.

**Sequencing:** Fed policy expectations move first, HIBOR and bank liquidity follow with a lag of a few weeks, and equity valuations react to both in real time. The valuation channel fires before the liquidity channel. That is the timing rule for any rate-driven HK call.

## The peg, worked cases (DURABLE as case studies)

The dated sequences are citable forever; the levels inside them are not.

- **The 2022 squeeze, the benchmark for any future drain.** The Aggregate Balance fell from over HK$340 billion to near HK$50 billion and sent one-month HIBOR to a 14-year high. Data: Reuters, November 2022.
- **June 2026, the valuation channel firing alone.** On June 17 the Fed held and nine of eighteen officials penciled in another hike; the HSI fell from about 23,400 to 22,671 between June 18 and 26, roughly 3%, with no local earnings news; on June 26 the HKD weakened to 7.8417, a ten-month low. Data: CNBC (dot plot), HKSAR Government press release (HKD), Investing.com (HSI).
- **July 2026, the chain running in reverse at equal speed.** The July 3 US jobs report showed 57,000 jobs added against 110,000 expected; the next session the Hang Seng rose roughly 3%, its best single day in weeks, undoing roughly a quarter of the June drop. The article calls this the cleanest proof in the sequence. Data: BLS release July 3 2026, Investing.com.

## The discount (DURABLE)

### GDP growth is not shareholder profit

The house rebuttal to "China grows X%, so buy China". The growth model is investment-heavy, credit-fueled and state-directed: a highways company doubles revenue building empty capacity, a bank multiplies its balance sheet by the credit multiplier, and distributable profits to minority holders do not follow.

### Part of the discount is earned

Three structural reasons, and the required counterweight in any bullish HK piece: cash trapped behind capital controls is worth less than cash that can leave, policy can rewrite a company's economics overnight, and the local habit of returning cash to shareholders has historically been weak. See [frames.md](frames.md) F5 and F6.

### Mandated is not contractual

The house answer to "Beijing mandated record payouts, why is HK still cheap?". A mandated dividend is worth less than a contractual one and the market discounts the gap. The multiple is not ignoring the payouts, it is pricing their reversibility.

**The precision distinction**, to deploy whenever comparing China's payout wave to the Japan and Korea re-rating: China raised payouts by command (CSRC instruction), whereas Japan and South Korea raised payouts over the past decade through governance reform, meaning independent boards, stewardship codes, price-to-book targets, and investor pressure the state enabled but did not command. The accurate label is state-directed capitalism with investor-friendly features, not shareholder primacy.

### What would close it

The standing checklist for any "what re-rates Hong Kong" section. Three conditions: durability (not existence) of shareholder returns proven across a full adverse cycle, a durable fix to the property sector, and a geopolitical thaw. Absent all three, a persistent discount is a stable outcome rather than a temporary bottom.

### The US comparison correction

Part of the S&P 500's headline multiple is an illusion of breadth: seven companies led by Apple, Microsoft and Nvidia make up about a third of the index by value, so stripping them out leaves the median American stock far cheaper than the index implies. The correction that stops any HK versus US multiple comparison being a story about seven stocks. The about-one-third weight is itself perishable (as_of June 2026).

## China policy timeline (DURABLE)

The documented record. Dates and events are fixed; see [open-questions.md](open-questions.md) item 3 on the September 2024 facility size.

- **2020, three red lines.** Limits on developer debt triggered the Evergrande, Country Garden and Sunac defaults, collapsing land sales and local-government revenue. The dateable origin of the property crisis.
- **2020 to 2021, the crackdown.** Ant Group's IPO halted, Alibaba antitrust fines, Tencent gaming revenue capped, Didi forced to delist after its NYSE listing, the for-profit K-12 education sector wiped out. The market's reading was structural: the state can rewrite rules retroactively. The standing citation for the regulatory-risk premium.
- **2018 to 2021, the index pivot.** The HSI moved from an old-economy book (HSBC, ICBC, BOC, Sun Hung Kai, CK Asset) to include Alibaba, Meituan, Xiaomi, Tencent and JD.com, with the Hang Seng Tech Index launched, landing the index into the crackdown within months. Why the index's tech exposure arrived at the worst possible moment.
- **2022 to 2023, China Special Valuation.** State banks, energy majors and telecom operators told to lift dividends and ROE.
- **April 2024, the State Council's Nine Measures.** Tightened dividend discipline, exposing persistent non-payers to special-treatment warnings and delisting pressure.
- **September 2024, the PBOC package.** A facility for institutions to buy equities plus a re-lending line financing corporate buybacks directly. Size disputed across two articles, see [open-questions.md](open-questions.md) item 3.
- **2024, execution.** A-share cash dividends above 2 trillion yuan and buybacks across more than a thousand listed companies. Proof the mandate is not rhetoric.
- **May to June 2026, the offshore-brokerage crackdown.** China's securities regulator and seven other agencies jointly announced a two-year crackdown on offshore brokerages serving mainland investors (Futu, Tiger Brokers, Longbridge), banning new purchases and allowing only sales and withdrawals. Futu alone faces roughly 1.85 billion yuan in fines; regulators estimate 200 to 250 billion yuan of mainland-linked HK holdings are affected. **A Fed-independent outflow channel: any HK flow analysis must separate it from peg mechanics.** Data: CNA, June 2026.
- **November 2025, the Trump-Xi Busan truce.** Cut the fentanyl-linked tariff from 20% to 10% and suspended further measures through November 2026. Both revocable. The tariff-state reference for any China or HK macro piece written before November 2026, and it expires then. Data: China Briefing, November 2025.
- **April 2 2026, the tariff step.** The US raised the effective tariff rate on Chinese goods to 31.6%. Data: US Trade Representative.
- **2025, US de minimis.** Washington scrapped the exemption Temu ran on.

## The 35-year trendline (DURABLE, with a live dispute)

The line drawn from the 1987 floor held through crisis lows at 1998, 2003, 2009 and 2016, then broke below for the first time in the index's history in October 2022, collapsing to 14,604. Data: TradingView.

**The touch count is not consistent anywhere in the source article.** The title says six bounces, the schema says five, the body says five and enumerates four, the chart alt text says five by counting the 2022 break as a low, and a risk callout says the line has never broken, contradicting the article's own thesis. The enumerated bounces are four. See [open-questions.md](open-questions.md) item 2 before writing any count.

**The default rule and the site's override.** Broken support becomes resistance, so a trendline retested from below historically favours rejection. That rule assumes nothing underneath has changed. The house position is that a trendline is only as valid as the economy it represents. Cite the position rather than re-arguing it.

## China's economic base (DURABLE, completed measurements)

The ammunition for the "economy under the line grew faster than the price above it" argument.

- China's GDP was $10.4 trillion in 2014 and $18.7 trillion in 2024, an 80% expansion in ten years.
- Nominal GDP went from $6.1 trillion (2010) to $19.4 trillion, 3.2x. Over the same span the S&P 500 went 1,258 to 5,450 (4.3x) and the Nikkei 10,250 to 38,000 (3.7x), while the Hang Seng closed December 2010 at around 23,000. The 2010 base values never change; the end values do. This is the site's strongest opening statistic.
- In 2024 China filed 1.8 million patents, nearly half the global total, for the fifth consecutive year, against 501,000 filed by the United States.
- China committed $49 billion to domestic AI semiconductors through the third National IC Fund, with a stated target of 80% chip self-sufficiency by 2030. A fixed, checkable goalpost.
- The Belt and Road Initiative deployed $121.8 billion in 2024, up 31%, across 149 countries, of which $21.4 billion went into metals and mining, a record.

Note: the 2010 to 2026 span is described as "16 years" four times in the GDP-paradox article. It is 15.5 years, and the S&P, Nikkei and GDP ratios are all quoted against it.

## The category error (DURABLE)

The house argument for HK-listed globals mispriced on a China label.

- CK Hutchison earns only 12% of its revenues from Greater China and its ports are in Rotterdam, Panama and Colombo. The China discount applied to a structurally global business.
- Jardine Matheson, CK Hutchison and Hongkong Land had no meaningful exposure to Chinese residential development and were sold anyway, indiscriminately, through the Evergrande contagion.

Verify the CK Hutchison revenue split against the latest FY before reuse.

### Named single-stock anchors from the macro pieces

These sit in macro articles rather than their own analyses. All perishable, all from [Cheap Is a Question, Not an Answer](../../publish/analyses/hong-kong-discount-cheap-two-ways.html) unless noted, prices June 11 to 19 2026, fundamentals from the latest full-year filing.

- **PAX Global (0327.HK):** about HK$3.62 a share in cash and short-term investments on end-2025 books, closed HK$3.47 on June 18. Cash exceeds the entire market value. The flagship net-cash example. The end-2025 cash per share is durable until the next filing.
- **CK Asset (1113.HK):** HK$44.66 on June 18, around 43% of book, earning about 2% on reinvested capital (below cost of capital), profit down more than a third over three years, no buyback, HK$44.3 billion UK power network sale recycled into new deals rather than returned. The canonical value-trap case; the 2% ROIC against cost of capital is the load-bearing number. The disposal and the no-buyback record are durable.
- **NetEase:** HK$190.50 on June 19, US$23.4 billion net cash (close to a third of market value), gaming priced at about 9x operating profit against Electronic Arts near 20x, US$5 billion buyback underway. The cleanest direct HK versus US same-industry comparison on the site.
- **Green Tea Group (6831.HK):** HK$6.35 on June 18, last-full-year sales +24% and profit +39%, against comparable Chinese restaurant chains at 17 to 22 times operating profit. The +24% and +39% are durable.
- **Yuexiu Property (0123.HK):** attributable profit fell about 95% last year and it still declared a dividend more than ten times what it earned.
- **Natural Food International (1837.HK):** net cash over a third of value, sales +22%, 65% gross margin, no debt, PepsiCo owns about 26%.
- **Water Oasis (1161.HK):** most of its value in cash, gross margin above 90%, insiders buying.
- **Emperor Watch and Jewellery:** strong cash generation, controlling family holding nearly 60%, a buyback mandate never used. The named warning case for F6. The "never used" claim expires the moment they use it.
- **Dickson Concepts (0113.HK):** HK$6.36 on June 11, about HK$2.73 billion net cash against roughly HK$2.35 billion market value. Full entry in the dedicated analysis.
- **Tencent:** more than HK$100 billion of buybacks in 2024 (gdp-paradox) / more than HK$120 billion returned last year (cheap-two-ways). See [open-questions.md](open-questions.md) item 5.
- **Alibaba:** authorized a $25 billion multi-year repurchase in 2023 and has kept executing it; fell from $230 to the $65 to $85 range through the crackdown. Both durable.
- **The Poon family / Dickson, 2025:** controlling 60.5%, offered to take the minority out at HK$7.20 against shares at HK$6.36, below asset value. The offer failed on a technicality and no buyback followed. The template case for lowball controller take-privates, and the site's worked example of "cheap, real, and locked". The reading of this event was reversed in June 2026: see [frames.md](frames.md) F2b.
