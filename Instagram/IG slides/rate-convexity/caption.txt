Hong Kong runs on a US toolkit that was built for a different market. Forty percent of the Hang Seng is financials, and the rate that moves them is set by the Fed, not by anything happening in Hong Kong.

Over the last six months the index fell double digits. Four of its five biggest banks rose anyway. Same market, opposite direction.

That gap is not luck. It is convexity, and right now it is pointing one specific way. The question is whether you are trading with it or against it.
