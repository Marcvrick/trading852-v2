China's economy tripled since 2010. Over the same 16 years the Hang Seng returned zero, while the S&P 500 rose 4.3x and the Nikkei nearly quadrupled.

The easy read is that the market is broken. The harder one is that it worked. It priced a growth story that never belonged to minority shareholders in the first place.

Then, quietly, something changed. Since 2022 Beijing started handing that growth back by command, and the index still trades at a third of the S&P's multiple. That discount is not pricing what most people think.
