Group Profit Up 21%. Shareholder Profit Down 94.7%. H2 Gross Margin: 4.33%.

Yuexiu's FY2025 announcement showed the headline profit going one way and the part reaching shareholders going the other. One line of accounting explains the gap. Underneath the full-year gross margin sits a second-half number that turns the cycle-recovery story on its head.

On 31 March 2026, Yuexiu Property reported FY2025 results that did not quite add up at first read. Group profit, the total across the whole company, rose 21.4% to RMB 1.78 billion. The slice reaching the parent company's shareholders fell 94.7%, to RMB 55 million. The gap sits in one line of the accounts. The partners who co-invest in Yuexiu's individual projects took RMB 1.72 billion of the total. That is four times what they took the year before.

Sixteen analysts rate the stock Buy at an average target of HKD 5.62. That is fifty percent above today's HKD 3.76. The buy case depends on a margin recovery the latest half-year of data has moved away from.

The cash flow is fine. Operating cash from the business was RMB 13.94 billion in 2025, roughly the size of the entire company at HKD 3.76. The accounting profit reaching shareholders is the problem.

What Yuexiu Property Does

Yuexiu Property builds and sells homes and offices in China's biggest cities. Its parent is Guangzhou Yuexiu Holdings, a state-owned company controlled by the Guangzhou municipal government. In 2025, Yuexiu was the ninth-largest residential developer in China by sales. Six core cities generated 85.6% of those sales: Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Hangzhou, Chengdu. Its land bank, the inventory of plots ready for future projects, totals 18.55 million square metres. 94.4% of it sits in tier-1 and tier-2 cities.

The company is still buying land. In 2025, it picked up 25 new plots across nine cities for RMB 24.40 billion, with 96.3% of that spending in the same six core cities. Many private competitors have stopped acquiring. Yuexiu is still adding to its pipeline in the markets that hold value best.

The bond market has noticed. Both Fitch and S&P now rate Yuexiu BBB- Stable, the bottom rung of investment grade and the highest rating any privately listed Chinese developer holds today. S&P added that rating during 2025. Fitch upgraded its outlook to Stable. The average rate Yuexiu pays to borrow fell by nearly half a percentage point, to 3.05%, well below the 5 to 6% range typical of weaker peers.

Why the Discount Exists

The HKD 3.76 price reflects three readings of the filing. The broader Chinese property cycle, with national home sales still around 25% below their 2021 peak. The headline 94.7% drop in shareholder profit, which most casual readers do not separate from the 21% rise in total profit. The gross margin path. The full-year gross margin of 7.81% looks like a stable bottom-of-cycle number compared to FY2024's 10.5%. It is not stable. It is the average of a 10.64% first half and a 4.33% second half. The trend inside the year went the wrong way.

The dividend tells the same story. The HKD 0.166 per share payment looks like a 4.4% yield. Against the company's shareholder profit, it is more than ten times what Yuexiu actually earned for shareholders that year. The dividend has already been cut. FY2024 paid HKD 0.189. FY2025 paid HKD 0.166. The board declared no final dividend in either year. This is the second consecutive year of falling distribution.

The credit story improved during 2025. The shareholder arithmetic got worse. The market is treating the second story as the more durable one.

The Recovery Story Has a Data Problem

The bullish case has a clear logic. Chinese developers do not book revenue when they sell a flat. They book it when they hand over the keys, which can take three years. So a flat in Guangzhou sold in 2022 only reaches the income statement in 2025, at the 2022 land cost against the 2024-25 selling price. Yuexiu and its peers have spent the past two years delivering homes built on land bought during 2020-2022, the most expensive batch ever. Those plots came from auctions when the market overheated and prices hit records. Once that batch clears, gross margin should rise toward the cost of land bought in 2023-24, when auction prices had fallen 30 to 40%.

The logic predicts margins should flatten or improve during 2025. The FY2025 filing shows the opposite. Gross margin in the first half was 10.64%, in line with FY2024 and consistent with the recovery story. Gross margin in the second half, calculated by subtracting the first-half figures from the full-year totals, was 4.33%. That is the worst single half on the disclosed record. The number that should signal the cycle is exiting moved the wrong way.

Two side disclosures soften the read but do not flip it. Contracted sales fell 7.3% to RMB 106.21 billion. These are homes sold but not yet delivered. The wider stock of homes already sold and waiting to be delivered into future revenue fell 22.4% to RMB 132.01 billion. The reservoir that would carry FY2026 revenue is smaller than the one that carried FY2025. The expensive batch is clearing. The next batch will be sold into a smaller order book.

Where the Profit Goes

The detail doing the heaviest lifting in FY2025 is how the profits get split between the parent company's shareholders and the partners who co-own each project. Group profit rose to RMB 1.78 billion in 2025, up from RMB 1.47 billion in 2024. Of that 2025 figure, RMB 1.72 billion went to project-level partners. Just RMB 55 million reached the parent company's shareholders. In 2024, the partners took RMB 425 million. In 2025, they took RMB 1.72 billion. Their share of group profit jumped from 29% to 97%.

This is normal in Chinese property. Developers form joint ventures at the project level with land owners, asset managers, and local-government investment vehicles. When a project completes profitably, Yuexiu's slice depends on how much of that specific project it owns. In 2024, the projects that delivered tended to be ones where Yuexiu owned the larger share. In 2025, the projects that delivered tended to be ones where Yuexiu owned the smaller share. That mix can swing either way in 2026.

The headline RMB 1.78 billion overstates what actually flowed to Yuexiu's shareholders. The RMB 55 million figure understates the operating result. The truer number is underlying net profit at RMB 261 million. This excludes one-off items but cannot adjust for the project mix.

What the Business Is Worth

The valuation works in scenarios because everything depends on whether the first half of 2026 recovers from the 4.33% gross margin floor of the second half of 2025. The base case is anchored more by the risk of further compression than by the analyst target.

The bull target of HKD 5.62 implies shareholder profit recovers to roughly RMB 1.5 to 2 billion in FY2026. From RMB 55 million in 2025, that means a thirty-fold rebound on the same revenue base. It also means both gross margin and project mix have to improve at the same time.

The investment-grade ratings, the cash position, and the state-owned parent all support the floor of the base case. None of them mechanically lifts the chance of recovery above where the second-half 2025 print sets it. Operating cash inflow of RMB 13.94 billion is what the business produces today. That figure is roughly the size of the entire company at HKD 3.76. It does not become accounting profit until the gross margin path resolves.

Risks We Are Not Downplaying

Risk 1: The margin compression is structural, not cycle-driven

If first-half 2026 gross margin prints below 8%, the recovery story is dead. The 4.33% second-half 2025 number suggests selling prices in today's softer market are eroding faster than land costs are coming down. In that scenario, FY2026 shareholder profit stays near zero. The next interim dividend would be at risk. The company's accumulated equity would shrink as losses pile up. The path resembles Country Garden's 2022-2023 sideways drift, but the state-owned parent prevents the second-leg cash crisis that hit private peers. The price level for that case is HKD 2.10 to 2.50.

Risk 2: Project-level partners keep absorbing most of the profit

The 97% partner share of group profit in FY2025 may not reverse on its own in FY2026. Yuexiu's joint-venture structure is heavy with state-affiliated investment funds and local development partners. They hold significant equity in the highest-margin projects. If the next batch of completing projects keeps tilting toward those where Yuexiu owns less, the gap between total profit and shareholder profit persists. Group profit could grow at 20% a year while shareholder profit stays marginal. The shareholders would own a parent company that controls operations but does not capture much of the upside on the printed numbers. Project mix is opaque to outsiders, so this is harder to forecast than a margin recovery.

A third factor sits in the background. Any selling or share-pledging by Guangzhou Yuexiu Holdings, the state-owned parent, would break the thesis. The investment-grade ratings from Fitch and S&P assume that backing stays in place. A parent stepping back, in any form, reverses the credit story and exposes the equity to a re-rating beyond the margin question.

The Decision

Written at HKD 3.76 on April 29, 2026.

The structural protections are real and getting better. RMB 13.94 billion of operating cash this year. Net debt at 54.9% of equity. The company sits within all of Beijing's debt limits for developers, the so-called Three Red Lines. New BBB- rating from S&P. Fitch outlook upgrade to Stable. Borrowing cost down by nearly half a percentage point. 94.4% of the land bank in tier-1 and tier-2 cities. None of those describe a company in distress.

The earnings story is harder. Total profit rose 21%, but almost all of the rise went to project-level partners. Shareholder profit collapsed 95%. Second-half 2025 gross margin of 4.33% was the worst single half on record. A thirty-fold rebound in shareholder profit during FY2026 is what the consensus target requires. Nothing in the FY2025 filing makes that more likely than it was a year ago.

The first-half 2026 results, due late August, are the test that resolves which of the two stories dominates.

Three signals would invalidate the watch entirely. A profit warning before the first-half 2026 results. A reduction or omission of the first-half 2026 dividend. Any HKEX filing showing Guangzhou Yuexiu Holdings reducing its stake or pledging its shares as collateral.

The credit profile is improving. The half of FY2025 in which margins should have firmed produced the worst gross margin number on record. The August 2026 release names which of the two stories is the dominant one.
