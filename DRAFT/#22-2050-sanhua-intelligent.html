Tesla's US$685M Order. Robot Revenue +320%. Filed Under Auto Parts.

Sanhua (2050.HK) makes the small parts that move heat in air conditioners, electric car battery packs, AI data centre cooling loops, and humanoid robot joints. The same supplier shows up in four growth waves.

Thermal Management Tesla Optimus AI Data Centre A+H Dual Listing HKEX Large-Cap

In October 2025, Tesla placed a US$685 million order for the actuators that move the joints of its Optimus humanoid robot. The supplier was a Chinese thermal components maker most readers will not have heard of. Sanhua is the sole assembler of those parts for Tesla, with delivery scheduled from a new Mexico factory in 2026. In the first half of 2025, Sanhua's robot revenue grew +320% year-on-year at a gross margin above 35%. The line is technically classified inside the company's auto parts segment in the consolidated revenue table. It is one of three concurrent growth waves the company is selling into.

FY2025 net profit grew +31%. Q1 2026 net profit grew +2.7%. The stock has rerated +75% from its 52-week low and now sits 22% below the high. Either Q1 was a seasonal pothole or the deceleration is structural.

What Sanhua Does

Zhejiang Sanhua Intelligent Controls (2050.HK), listed on Hong Kong's HKEX since June 2025 as a secondary listing alongside its existing Shenzhen A-share (002050.SZ), is the world's largest manufacturer of thermal management components. These are the small parts that move heat: expansion valves and four-way reversing valves in air conditioners, integrated thermal modules in electric vehicle thermal management systems, liquid cooling loops in AI data centres, and now actuators in robot joints.

In FY2025, revenue reached RMB 31.0 billion (+11.0%) and net profit reached RMB 4.06 billion (+31.1%). Gross margin expanded to 28.78%, up from 27.27% the year before and from roughly 25.6% three years earlier. The company holds RMB 10.9 billion in net cash, equal to about 8% of its market value. Operating cash flow ran at RMB 5.09 billion, well above net profit.

The product range used to fit on one line: refrigeration and air conditioning. Today it spans three additional verticals. Each shares the same engineering platform. Each is growing at a different rate.

Key Takeaway

The premium is the topic. Sanhua trades at 30× trailing earnings and 37–42× on broker forward FY2026 estimates, against Modine at 14× and a restructured BorgWarner at 21×. The math holds only if two of three new growth lines (electric vehicle thermal, AI data centre cooling, robot actuators) move from small to material inside 18 months. The first one is already 48% of revenue. The second and third have not yet shown up in segment-level reporting.

Why Sanhua Trades at a Premium

On trailing earnings, Sanhua sits at roughly 30× P/E. On Hong Kong broker forward estimates for FY2026 (Futunn at 37–42×, UOB Kay Hian initiated coverage with a Buy on dominant >55% market share), the multiple is 37–42×. Modine, the closest comparable on the AI data centre side, trades near 14× forward. BorgWarner trades near 21× on forward earnings, distorted by an ongoing post-electric-vehicle restructuring that depresses its earnings base. Hanon Systems, the Korean pure-play on electric vehicle thermal, is operating at net losses and is not meaningfully comparable.

What the premium needs to reflect: a business that grows faster than its peers (+11% revenue in FY2025 against a peer median near +2%), at margins that expand structurally rather than cyclically, with three separate growth lines that each compound at a different rate. The math does not support a 40× forward multiple on the auto parts segment alone. It supports it only if the data centre cooling and robot actuator lines move from small to material inside the next 18 months.

The IPO multiple was 26.7× trailing earnings. The market has pushed it higher.

The Three Growth Lines

Three observable signals support the premium. None is hypothetical. All three are already in reported revenue.

  1. 01 Electric vehicle thermal management is now 48% of revenue, growing at +35% in H1 2025. A year earlier, the segment was 44% of revenue. The shift is mechanical: an electric car uses two to three times more thermal components than an internal combustion vehicle. Sanhua supplies BYD, NIO, Li Auto on the domestic side, and BMW and Mercedes on the global side, via its Castel acquisition in Europe. The gross margin profile of electric vehicle thermal is roughly 28%, well above the 18% in the legacy refrigeration business. As segment mix shifts toward auto, blended margin expands.
  2. 02 Data centre liquid cooling generated roughly RMB 2 billion in FY2025. Liquid cooling becomes structurally necessary at the AI server power density that hyperscalers are deploying through 2027. Sanhua is one of a handful of Chinese suppliers qualified for hyperscaler liquid cooling loops. The revenue line is small today. If it grows at the pace of hyperscaler capex (roughly +30% per year through 2027), it reaches RMB 4–5 billion by FY2027. That is the optionality the multiple is paying for.
  3. 03 Robot actuator revenue grew +320% in H1 2025 at a gross margin above 35%. Tesla's October 2025 order (US$685M, roughly RMB 5 billion) names Sanhua as the sole assembler for Optimus joint actuators. Delivery from the Mexico factory begins in 2026. The actuator segment was effectively zero two years ago. FY2025 is the first material year. The margin is structurally higher than auto parts because the parts are bespoke and the qualification cycle was 18 months long.

What Sanhua Is Actually Worth

The pricing math is the inverse of every other article on this site. There is no obvious discount to close. There is a premium to defend.

Hong Kong broker consensus puts FY2026E net profit between RMB 4.66 and 4.83 billion. Applied to the high end of their forward multiple range (42×), the math produces a market value near RMB 203 billion. At the low end (37×), the figure is RMB 173 billion. Translated to per-share, the 12-month range falls between HK$42 and HK$48.

From HK$36.18, that is +16% to +33%. The stock has rerated +9% over the past three weeks and now sits 22% below the 52-week high (HK$46.48). The margin of safety has thinned but the broker consensus target has not been reached.

The downside stress case is more instructive than the upside. If the data centre cooling and robot lines remain unrecognised by the market and Sanhua de-rates to Modine's 14× forward multiple, the math produces HK$17–18 per share. That is the floor at which the company is priced as a Chinese auto parts supplier and nothing more.

One concrete frame for the robot optionality. Hong Kong brokers have begun modelling the segment explicitly. At 1 million Optimus units per year (Tesla's stated 2030 ambition), an average actuator price of RMB 50 000 per unit, and Sanhua holding 70% market share in the assembled actuator, the segment generates roughly RMB 3.5 billion in profit. That is 75% of FY2025 net profit, from one product line that did not exist three years ago. The number is conditional on a production trajectory the market is still discounting.

Risks We Are Not Downplaying

Two risks are documented. Neither is hypothetical.

Risk 1: Chinese OEM pricing pressure on electric vehicle thermal

BYD, NIO, and Li Auto cut supplier prices by 3 to 8 per cent every year. Hanon Systems, Sanhua's Korean peer, lived through the same dynamic with Hyundai and Kia and is now operating at net losses. The electric vehicle thermal segment is 48% of Sanhua's revenue and carries gross margin near 28%. Every percentage point of OEM-driven margin compression flows directly through the blended margin. If electric vehicle thermal gross margin falls below 25% across two consecutive half-year reports, the thesis on structural margin expansion is broken and the multiple no longer holds.

Risk 2: Q1 2026 confirms the H2 2025 deceleration

Q1 2026 revenue grew only +1.4% year-on-year. Net profit grew +2.7%. The legacy refrigeration division contracted -6% in revenue and -12% in profit, as Chinese home appliance subsidies wound down. The auto and electric vehicle division still grew +15%, and gross margin held at 27.8%. But the headline +31% FY2025 growth is now firmly behind the company. The H1 2026 result, due in August 2026, will show whether the slowdown is a one-quarter subsidy artefact or the beginning of a real deceleration. If H1 2026 net profit growth comes in below +15% year-on-year, a forward multiple of 37–42× is no longer defensible.

The Decision

Written at HK$36.18 on May 22, 2026.

The thesis is platform reuse. The same engineering capability that ships expansion valves to air conditioning OEMs ships actuators to Tesla. The same workforce that produces battery thermal modules for BYD produces liquid cooling loops for hyperscalers. Three growth lines, one cost structure.

The setup is not asymmetric in the classical sense. The upside to a 12-month broker consensus target of HK$42–48 is +16% to +33%. The downside in a peer-multiple de-rating, with the data centre and robot lines unrecognised, is to HK$17–18 ($-$50%). The stock has moved +9% over the past three weeks alone.

Three signals invalidate the thesis. Electric vehicle thermal gross margin below 25% across two consecutive half-year reports. Revenue growth below +8% across a full year (FY2026 or FY2027). The principal A-share holder (Sanhua Green Energy, which holds 15.78% of the float) announcing a fresh pledge or new exchangeable bond against Sanhua shares. The original overhang resolved in September 2025 when the existing exchangeable bond converted in full. A new one would suggest the controlling family is monetising at the expense of equity holders, which is a different problem from operating performance.

The company has the products. The question is whether the price already discounts a future where two of three new growth lines work, and what is left if only one does.
