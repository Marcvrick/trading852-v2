Pop Mart +185%. Budweiser China -9.2%. One Average Hides Both.

China's headline consumer number does not tell you whether you are looking at the right half of the market. The same geography produced a 200-point revenue spread across its consumer subsectors in 2025. The two halves are not in the same economic cycle.

China's total retail sales grew 3.7% in full-year 2025. That figure circulated widely as evidence of a recovering consumer. The same year, Pop Mart grew revenue 184.7%. Laopu Gold grew 221%. ANTA Sports grew 13.3%. Budweiser watched its China volumes fall 9.2%. Haidilao's same-store sales declined 6.7%. Chow Tai Fook posted same-store declines of 19.4% before any recovery began. The 3.7% is not wrong. It is the mean of two distributions with opposite signs.

The distribution matters more than the mean.

What the government bet on

China's consumer confidence averaged 89 points on the NBS scale throughout 2025, well below the 100 neutral mark and far from the 110–115 levels that prevailed before 2022. Beijing responded with its largest-ever consumer stimulus package: CNY 300 billion allocated in 2025 via ultra-long special treasury bonds, double the 2024 budget. The program covered home appliances, automobiles, and smartphones. It drove related merchandise sales exceeding CNY 2.6 trillion across the year and contributed over one percentage point to retail growth.

The stimulus worked where it was aimed. Appliance sales surged more than 38% year-on-year in Q4 2024. Auto trade-ins exceeded 6.8 million units in 2024. A separate Special Action Plan to Boost Consumption, issued on March 16, 2026, added 30 measures across eight policy categories. In December 2025, Beijing pre-loaded CNY 62.5 billion for the 2026 program before the year had started.

The government doubled its consumer stimulus budget. The three fastest-growing subsectors were not eligible for a single yuan.

The split is not random. The three winning subsectors sell experiences, identity, and domestic premium alternatives to international brands. The three declining subsectors sell volume-dependent products to price-sensitive consumers. Government stimulus reached the second group and not the first.

What is driving the gap

The winning subsectors are not growing because consumers feel wealthier. Consumer confidence remained below 90 throughout 2025. The growth is identity-driven: a specific cohort of Chinese consumers is redirecting spend away from international luxury brands and commodity-priced consumption, toward domestic premium experiences with cultural specificity.

Laopu Gold sells ancient-craft (古法) gold jewelry at a 2–3x premium over mainstream jewelers. Revenue rose from HKD 8.5 billion in FY2024 to HKD 27.3 billion in FY2025. Same-store sales grew 160.6% for the year and 201% in the first half. Its listed competitor, Chow Tai Fook, posted same-store declines of 19.4% in the same period. Both companies sell gold. The product is not the same product.

Pop Mart's IP catalog generated 184.7% revenue growth with a 35.1% net margin and a 72.1% gross margin expanding for the third consecutive year. Overseas revenue exceeded 40% of total for the first time, with stores across Southeast Asia, Europe, and the United States. The company's LABUBU character became a global cultural export. Its comparative benchmark, Funko in the United States and Bandai in Japan, grew near-zero over the same period.

ANTA Sports grew 13.3% against a clothing retail market growing 3.2%. Its four-brand architecture operates at 62% gross margin, structurally above Nike's 44%. The brand holds 23% of China sportswear market share. The key risk is FILA, ANTA's premium segment, whose revenue direction in H1 2026 will determine whether the multi-brand model compounds further or faces mix compression.

The declining subsectors share a different profile. Budweiser's China volumes fell 9.2% in FY2025, revenue 12.7%: the third consecutive year of decline in the primary market. The company carries USD 2.5 billion in net cash and a 50.1% gross margin. Neither asset insulates it from the structural demand shift. An aging population and a younger cohort preferring spirits, tea-based RTDs, and local craft beer are not cyclical phenomena. The company paid a dividend exceeding 156% of net income, funded from the cash pile, not earnings. Haidilao's table turnover fell from 4.1 to 3.9 times per day. Same-store sales declined 6.7% for the year, with H1 down 9.9%. Revenue grew 1.1%, driven entirely by new store openings: the company closed 51 self-operated locations and opened 66 franchise units during the year. Chow Tai Fook's fiscal year 2025 ended with mainland China same-store sales down 19.4%. The first half of fiscal year 2026 showed early stabilization: mainland self-operated stores turned positive at +2.6% and franchised at +4.8%. That recovery is real. It is not yet confirmation of a trend.

What is priced in and what is not

The bifurcation in fundamentals has not fully transferred into relative valuations. The winning subsectors trade at discounts to global peers. The struggling subsectors trade at multiples that assume recoveries not yet confirmed by data.

ANTA at 11.56x EV/EBIT represents a 47% discount to the global sportswear peer average of 22x. The discount reflects the China risk premium applied uniformly across all HKEX consumer names. It does not reflect ANTA's 62% gross margin, 21.4% FCF return on capital employed, or the fact that its free cash flow of HKD 18.3 billion grew 38% in FY2025 while reported net income fell due to a one-time base effect from the prior year's Amer Sports IPO gains. JNBY Design trades at 9.22x EV/EBIT versus its peer average of 13.7x, paying 8.7% dividend yield on a 65.6% gross margin fashion business with 27.7% ROIC and net cash.

On the other side: Chow Tai Fook at 14.81x EV/EBIT commands a 65% premium over its actual Chinese jewelry peers, Chow Sang Sang (approximately 10x) and Luk Fook (approximately 8x). Three consecutive years of near-flat revenue and a gold inventory position at 2.1 times equity support that premium only if the recovery trajectory in fiscal year 2026 is confirmed and sustained. The market is paying a quality multiple for a business waiting for volume to return.

ANTA is cheap for an identifiable, quantifiable reason. Chow Tai Fook is expensive for a theoretical one.

The risks that do not discriminate

Risk 1: Gold price correlation

Both winning and losing jewelers carry levered gold exposure. Laopu's HKD 16 billion in inventory at 344 days outstanding is a leveraged position in sustained gold prices. A 20% correction writes down HKD 3.2 billion, wiping 65% of net equity. Chow Tai Fook carries HKD 55.4 billion in gold inventory against HKD 26.4 billion in equity: a 2.1x leverage ratio. Gold above USD 2,800 per ounce sustained both stories in 2025. China's gold and jewelry retail grew 12.8% in value terms over the same period, while volume in units fell 32.5% according to World Gold Council data for Q1–Q3 2025: the price per gram is carrying the revenue figures, not consumer volume. Any sustained reversal in gold prices hits both sides of the jewellery subsector simultaneously and does not distinguish between premium and mainstream positioning.

Risk 2: IP concentration at Pop Mart

Pop Mart's 184.7% revenue growth is not distributed evenly across its twelve active IP characters. LABUBU is the primary driver and the basis for the bear case: the character's 2024–2025 virality in Southeast Asia mirrors short-cycle consumer crazes with well-documented normalization patterns. Overseas revenue exceeding 40% of total provides partial geographic diversification. The business case for Pop Mart as a platform, not a single-character bet, rests on one observable fact: Molly, launched in 2016, still generates meaningful revenue nine years later. The critical test is H1 2026 results (August 2026). Revenue above HKD 14 billion would confirm the platform hypothesis. Revenue below HKD 10 billion would confirm the cycle hypothesis. The market has already priced the concern into a forward P/E of 9.81x: one of the lowest multiples for a business with a 35.1% net margin in any market.

What the split implies

Three events in August–September 2026 will confirm or challenge the bifurcation thesis.

Scenario Observable signal (August 2026) Implication
Bifurcation confirms ANTA FILA segment stable · Pop Mart H1 rev > HKD 14B · Haidilao SSSG stays negative Discount narrows in premium subsectors; multiple compression accelerates in declining ones. Thesis intact.
Mass market recovers CTF SSS sustains above +3% · Haidilao turnover recovers toward 4.1x · Budweiser H1 China volume positive Volume-dependent plays re-rate. Premium subsectors grow into current multiples. Split narrows.
Gold shock Gold price sustained below USD 2,300 per ounce · Laopu inventory write-down above HKD 2 billion Entire jewellery subsector de-rates regardless of brand trajectory. Thesis paused, not invalidated.

Beijing's trade-in program bought measurable results in appliances and automobiles. It did not rescue volume-dependent consumer businesses and it did not create the premium experiential boom. Budweiser's China volumes will not recover because consumers received appliance vouchers. Haidilao's table turnover will not return to 4.1x through macroeconomic stimulus. The recovery in those businesses requires a genuine shift in consumer spending patterns, not a policy directive.

The winning subsectors need no rescue. ANTA's H1 2026 FILA data is the single number that determines whether 9x EV/EBITDA is a value trap or a once-in-a-decade entry. Pop Mart's H1 2026 revenue determines whether LABUBU was a supercycle or a proof of concept. JNBY's inventory days at 178 and trending up will show, before the end of 2026, whether the 65.6% gross margin survives a markdowns cycle or not.

China's retail headline will likely reach 4–5% in 2026. It will obscure the same split it obscured in 2025. The government is doubling down on stimulus for the categories it knows how to reach. The categories it cannot reach are growing faster than anything the program covers.

The 3.7% average conceals a clear choice. Both halves carry China risk. Only one is priced as though China risk is the only story.
