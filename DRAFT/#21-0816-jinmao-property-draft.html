Key Takeaway

Jinmao Property Services (0816.HK) closed at HKD 2.47 on June 11, 2026. The company holds HKD 1.9 billion in cash. Its total market value is HKD 2.2 billion. The operating business, the one that manages buildings, collects fees, and paid a 9.5% dividend last year, costs HKD 350 million. The same business earned HKD 475 million in operating profit in 2025.

The operating business itself costs you HKD 0.39 per share. That same operating business earned HKD 0.53 in profit per share last year.

The stock has traded between HKD 2.30 and HKD 3.50 over the past twelve months. At HKD 2.47, buyers are collecting a 9.5% dividend while they wait.

What Jinmao Does

Jinmao Property Services manages residential and mixed-use buildings across mainland China. It handles the things residents never want to think about: security guards at the entrance, cleaners in the corridors, maintenance crews fixing the lifts, groundskeepers in the courtyards. It does not build property or own any of it. It earns a fixed monthly fee per square metre, paid by residents, for keeping buildings functional.

This is not the part of the Chinese real estate chain that anyone spends time on. The developers get the headlines. The property managers get the cleaning contracts.

In 2025, Jinmao managed 83.5 million square metres. Revenue grew +7.4% and the company generated HKD 800 million in free cash flow: the cash left after paying every bill and every cost of running the business. The stock pays a 9.5% dividend at today's price. The balance sheet holds HKD 1.9 billion in cash, accumulated over three years of consistent generation.

Why the Stock Is Cheap

The parent company is the reason. China Jinmao Holdings Group is the controlling shareholder of Jinmao Property. It is also a mainland property developer. And like most mainland property developers, it has had a difficult few years: sales volumes down, financing tighter, balance sheet under pressure.

That creates one specific worry for anyone looking at the subsidiary. A parent company that is short on cash holds a profitable subsidiary sitting on HKD 1.9 billion. Will the parent use connected transactions, intercompany fees, or dividend suppression to move some of that cash upward? The market does not know the answer. So it discounts the stock as though the answer is yes.

The second issue is that gross profit margins have been declining. In 2023, the margin on every fee collected was above 20%. By 2025 it was 19.1%. Labour costs have risen. The higher-margin services have grown more slowly than the basic cleaning-and-security work. It is a real trend, not noise.

The cash is on the balance sheet. The question is whether the parent will let it stay there.

Three Things That Move the Needle

None of the three requires the business to become something different. The business already does what it does. These are conditions that clarify who the cash belongs to.

One. The H1 2026 results, due in August. They will show whether the gross margin has stabilised above 19% or continued to slide. Above 19%: the compression story stalls. Below 17%: the profit structure changes and the dividend capacity shrinks. This is the most concrete near-term check.

Two. Parent restructuring. China Jinmao Holdings is managing its balance sheet actively. A formal restructuring that cleanly separates the parent's debts from the subsidiary's cash would remove the central worry. The discount on the subsidiary is priced on today's relationship. A documented separation prices a different one.

Three. A special dividend. The ordinary dividend already pays 9.5%. A special distribution would do one thing no analyst note can do: confirm in a single transaction that the cash on the balance sheet belongs to shareholders, not to the parent's war chest.

What a Fair Price Looks Like

The ratio of what you pay for the business versus what it earns annually is 0.74 today. In plain English: the operating business costs HKD 350 million and earned HKD 475 million in operating profit last year. Every comparable property manager in Hong Kong trades at a higher ratio than that.

Poly Property Services, which faces similar pressures from a state-owned parent, trades at 1.8 times. Greentown Services trades at 1.5 times. CR Mixc Lifestyle, the sector's premium brand with no parent stress, trades at 15.4 times. Jinmao is below all three. It is the cheapest listed property manager by this measure.

If Jinmao simply re-rates to where Poly Property trades today, 1.8 times, the implied share price is near HKD 3.00. That is +21% from today's price, using a multiple that a directly comparable company already carries. No turnaround required. No premium. Just parity with a peer in a similar situation.

If the governance discount eases further and the multiple moves toward 5 times, the implied price exceeds HKD 4.50. The 9.5% dividend pays investors while they wait for either outcome.

The Risks Worth Taking Seriously

The parent extracts the cash

This is the central risk. If China Jinmao Holdings uses the subsidiary to fund its own obligations, through inflated intercompany fees, forced loans, or asset transfers, the HKD 1.9 billion that underlies the valuation case starts to leave. The thesis depends on that cash staying where it is. If a connected transaction above HKD 460 million appears in the FY2026 filings without a clear business reason, that is the signal the thesis is failing.

Gross margin falls below 17%

The current 19.1% margin is the floor that keeps the dividend sustainable. Below 17%, operating profit compresses, the dividend shrinks, and the business looks like a different company than the one described here. The H1 2026 result in August is the first real check. If the number prints below 17%, reassess everything.

The receivables are not as clean as they look

The company is owed roughly HKD 390 million in outstanding payments from clients. That is a significant number relative to the annual net profit. If a portion of that is owed by parent-related entities and proves unrecoverable, the cash position that anchors the valuation is overstated. The counterparty breakdown has not been fully disclosed.

The Decision

Written at HKD 2.47 on June 11, 2026.

The stock pays 9.5% while you wait. The business earns back what you paid for it in less than a year. The cash on the balance sheet is larger than everything the market says the company is worth. Those three facts are already true.

What is not yet true is the fourth fact the thesis needs: confirmation that the cash serves shareholders, not the parent. That confirmation has a known first test date in August 2026.
